# The Constitutional Convention
APUSH - Unit 3

---

## Why Did We Need a New Constitution?

The **Articles of Confederation** had major problems:

- Weak central government
- No power to tax
- No executive branch
- Required unanimous consent for amendments

---

## Philadelphia, 1787

**When:** May - September 1787

**Where:** Independence Hall, Philadelphia

**Who:** 55 delegates from 12 states (Rhode Island didn't attend)

---

## Key Figures

**George Washington** - Presiding officer

**James Madison** - "Father of the Constitution"

**Benjamin Franklin** - Eldest delegate (81 years old)

**Alexander Hamilton** - Strong central government advocate

---

## The Virginia Plan

Proposed by **James Madison**:

- Three branches of government
- Bicameral legislature
- Representation based on **population**

**Who supported it?** Large states (Virginia, Pennsylvania, Massachusetts)

---

## The New Jersey Plan

Proposed by **William Paterson**:

- Unicameral legislature
- **Equal representation** for all states
- More power to states

**Who supported it?** Small states (New Jersey, Delaware, Connecticut)

---

## The Great Compromise

Created by **Roger Sherman** (Connecticut Compromise):

**House of Representatives:**
- Based on population
- Favored large states

**Senate:**
- Equal representation (2 per state)
- Favored small states

**Result:** Both sides got something they wanted

---

## The Three-Fifths Compromise

**The Problem:** Should enslaved people count for representation?

**North:** No, they're not citizens

**South:** Yes, for representation (but not voting)

**Compromise:** Enslaved people counted as 3/5 of a person for:
- Representation in House
- Direct taxation

---

## Slave Trade Compromise

**Northern states:** Wanted to ban slave trade immediately

**Southern states:** Needed slave labor for economy

**Compromise:**
- Congress could NOT ban slave trade until 1808
- Congress could tax imported enslaved people

---

## Separation of Powers

Three branches to prevent tyranny:

**Legislative** (Congress) - Makes laws

**Executive** (President) - Enforces laws

**Judicial** (Supreme Court) - Interprets laws

---

## Checks and Balances

Each branch can limit the others:

- President can **veto** laws
- Congress can **override** veto (2/3 vote)
- Supreme Court can declare laws **unconstitutional**
- Senate confirms presidential appointments

---

## Federalism

Power divided between:

**National Government:**
- Declare war
- Print money
- Regulate interstate commerce

**State Governments:**
- Education
- Local laws
- Elections

---

## Ratification Debate

**Federalists** (supported Constitution):
- Strong central government needed
- Checks and balances prevent tyranny
- Led by Hamilton, Madison, Jay

**Anti-Federalists** (opposed):
- Too much power to central government
- No Bill of Rights
- Led by Patrick Henry, George Mason

---

## The Federalist Papers

85 essays written by:
- Alexander Hamilton (51)
- James Madison (29)
- John Jay (5)

**Purpose:** Convince New York to ratify

**Most famous:** Federalist No. 10 (Madison)

---

## Ratification Process

**Required:** 9 of 13 states to ratify

**First to ratify:** Delaware (Dec 7, 1787)

**Ninth state:** New Hampshire (June 21, 1788)

**Last holdouts:** North Carolina, Rhode Island

---

## The Bill of Rights

**Promise:** Add amendments protecting individual rights

**When:** Ratified December 15, 1791

**What:** First 10 amendments

**Why:** Convinced Anti-Federalists to support Constitution

---

## Key Takeaways

1. Constitutional Convention replaced Articles of Confederation
2. Great Compromise created bicameral legislature
3. Three-Fifths Compromise counted enslaved people
4. Three branches with checks and balances
5. Bill of Rights added to protect individual freedoms
