<!-- .slide: data-background-color="#f3e9d7" -->
<h1 class="r-fit-text">3.1 British Empire & Colonial Society</h1>

- Political culture shaped by **English rights** and **local assemblies**
- Colonial society is diverse: **class**, **race**, **region**
- Salutary neglect allows local autonomy to grow

---

<!-- .slide: data-background-color="#efe2cf" -->
<h1 class="r-fit-text">3.2 Transatlantic Trade & Regional Economies</h1>

- **New England:** shipping, shipbuilding, rum, commerce
- **Middle Colonies:** wheat, mixed farming, urban trade
- **Southern Colonies:** plantation agriculture, enslaved labor
- Mercantilism ties colonies to British markets and rules

---

<!-- .slide: data-background-color="#f3e9d7" -->
<h1 class="r-fit-text">3.3 Imperial Wars & Consequences</h1>

- **French and Indian War (1754–1763)** reshapes North America
- Britain gains territory, but also **massive debt**
- Native alliances and land claims intensify conflict

---

<!-- .slide: data-background-color="#efe2cf" -->
<h1 class="r-fit-text">3.4 Imperial Reforms & Colonial Resistance</h1>

- **Proclamation of 1763** limits westward settlement
- **Revenue acts** (Stamp, Townshend, Tea) enforce taxation
- Colonists argue **"no taxation without representation"**

---

<h1 class="r-fit-text">Cause → Effect Chain</h1>

- War debt → British taxation
- Taxation → colonial protest and boycotts
- Protest → increased British enforcement
- Enforcement → deeper colonial unity

---

<h1 class="r-fit-text">Timeline Snapshot</h1>
